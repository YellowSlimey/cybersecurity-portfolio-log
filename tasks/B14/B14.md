## Teach Friends About a Cybersecurity Topic of Your Choice

### Description

For this task, I taught a friend about Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS) and how they are used in cybersecurity to detect suspicious activity inside networks. I explained the difference between Network IDS (NIDS) and Host IDS (HIDS), as well as the differences between signature-based and anomaly-based detection methods. I also discussed how IDS differs from IPS and used real-world examples and analogies to make the concepts easier to understand.

### Reflection

This activity helped me realise that teaching cybersecurity concepts is very different from simply understanding them personally. Explaining IDS concepts to someone without a technical background forced me to simplify ideas and think carefully about how to communicate them clearly. Using analogies and real-world examples made the discussion much more engaging and helped my friend understand the purpose of intrusion detection systems more easily. Overall, the experience improved both my confidence in discussing cybersecurity topics and my ability to explain technical concepts in a more accessible way.
