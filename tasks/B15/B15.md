## Teach an Elderly Person About a Cybersecurity Topic of Your Choice

### Description

For this task, I created and presented a PowerPoint presentation about phishing scams and online safety for an elderly audience (My Grandpa) in person. The presentation explained what phishing is, common warning signs of scams, how attackers manipulate victims, and practical ways to stay safe online. The session used simple language, real-world examples, and visual elements such as icons, screenshots, and diagrams to make the information easier to understand and remember.

### Reflection

This activity showed me how important clear communication is when explaining cybersecurity concepts to non-technical audiences. Teaching elderly users required simplifying technical ideas into practical, easy-to-understand advice without using complicated terminology. I also realised that phishing attacks rely heavily on emotional manipulation, such as creating urgency, fear, or panic to pressure victims into making mistakes. Using visual elements throughout the presentation made the information much easier to follow and helped keep the discussion engaging. Overall, the experience improved my ability to explain cybersecurity topics clearly and adapt technical content for different audiences.
