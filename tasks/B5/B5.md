## Attend 2 Cybersecurity Related Talks/Seminars

### Description

This task involved attending and reflecting on two cybersecurity-related talks/seminars focused on modern threat detection and AI security. The sessions provided insight into real-world defensive security practices, intrusion detection workflows, and emerging risks associated with artificial intelligence systems.

### Seminar 1 — Network Threat Hunting With Zeek and Suricata  
**Presenter:** Mark Overholser  
**Format:** Online technical presentation (YouTube)

#### Summary

This presentation focused on practical network threat hunting using two widely used open-source security monitoring tools: **Zeek** and **Suricata**.

The talk explained how organisations use network telemetry and intrusion detection systems (IDS) to identify suspicious behaviour inside enterprise environments. Zeek was presented as a powerful network analysis framework that generates detailed logs of network activity, while Suricata was demonstrated as a signature-based IDS/IPS capable of inspecting live traffic for known malicious patterns.

Key topics covered included:
- Network visibility and traffic monitoring
- Intrusion detection systems (IDS)
- Signature-based threat detection
- Log analysis and threat hunting workflows
- Detecting lateral movement and suspicious behaviour
- Real-world use of Zeek and Suricata in Security Operations Centres (SOCs)

The presentation also demonstrated how analysts investigate alerts and correlate network events to identify potential compromise inside a network.

#### What I Learned

This session improved my understanding of how network monitoring tools are used operationally in cybersecurity environments. I learned the difference between traffic inspection and behavioural analysis, and how tools like Zeek and Suricata complement each other during threat hunting activities. It also reinforced concepts related to IDS systems that I later explored further in Task B14.

### Seminar 2 — AI Security Lecture (Week 12)  
**Presenter:** Larry Huynh  
**Format:** University lecture/seminar

#### Summary

This lecture explored security risks, vulnerabilities, and emerging challenges associated with artificial intelligence systems. The session focused on how AI technologies introduce new attack surfaces and security considerations beyond traditional software systems.

Topics discussed included:
- Prompt injection attacks
- AI jailbreak techniques
- Model misuse and adversarial prompting
- Risks of overreliance on generative AI
- AI-generated misinformation and deepfakes
- Security implications of deploying large language models
- Ethical concerns surrounding AI systems

The lecture also discussed the importance of implementing safeguards, monitoring mechanisms, and secure deployment practices for AI-assisted systems.

#### What I Learned

This lecture helped me understand that AI security is not only about protecting AI models themselves, but also about protecting users and organisations from the unintended consequences of AI misuse. I learned how generative AI systems can be manipulated through prompt engineering and why AI systems require additional layers of security and validation.

### Reflection

Attending these two cybersecurity talks expanded my understanding of both traditional network security and emerging AI-related threats.
